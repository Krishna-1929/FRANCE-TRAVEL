Ratatouille

Ratatouille is a classic French Provençal dish made with a colorful medley of vegetables such as eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, onions, and tomatoes. This rustic and hearty dish is a celebration of the region's agricultural bounty.

Cooked slowly to allow the flavors to meld together, ratatouille can be served as a main course, side dish, or even a topping for crusty bread. Its rich flavors and wholesome ingredients make it a versatile and beloved part of French cuisine.

Ratatouille’s origins are rooted in the farms of Provence, where it was traditionally a simple meal for farmers. Today, it has become a globally recognized symbol of French culinary artistry.
