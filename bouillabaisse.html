Bouillabaisse

Bouillabaisse is a traditional Provençal fish stew originating from the coastal city of Marseille. This hearty dish combines various types of fresh Mediterranean fish, shellfish, and aromatic herbs with a saffron-flavored broth.

The dish is served with toasted bread and a flavorful rouille sauce, adding richness to its taste. Its origin lies in the fishing communities of Marseille, where it began as a humble meal made with leftover fish.

Bouillabaisse has evolved into a celebrated gourmet dish and is a quintessential part of Provençal cuisine. The experience of savoring it in Marseille is enhanced by the ambiance of the Mediterranean coastline.
