Experience at Gorges du Verdon

Gorges du Verdon, often called the "Grand Canyon of Europe," is a breathtaking natural wonder located in the Provence region of southern France. Known for its striking turquoise waters, steep cliffs, and dramatic landscapes, it offers a variety of outdoor activities for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Whether you enjoy hiking, kayaking, or simply taking in the stunning views, Gorges du Verdon is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the beauty of Provence.

The Gorges du Verdon is a 25 km-long canyon carved by the Verdon River. The deep blue-green waters of the river contrast sharply with the surrounding limestone cliffs, creating an awe-inspiring scene. The canyon is also a popular destination for kayakers and boaters who want to explore its unique landscape from the water.

The Verdon River flows through the canyon, offering visitors the opportunity to kayak, canoe, or take boat tours along the water. The river's crystal-clear waters are surrounded by towering cliffs, making it one of the most picturesque spots in France.

For those who prefer land-based activities, the Gorges du Verdon offers numerous hiking trails. The routes vary in difficulty, but all offer spectacular views of the canyon and surrounding landscape. The most famous hike is the Sentier Blanc-Martel, which takes you through the heart of the canyon and offers some of the most iconic vistas in the area.

The area is also home to several charming villages, such as Moustiers-Sainte-Marie, which sits at the foot of the cliffs. The village is known for its picturesque streets and stunning views of the canyon. It’s a perfect place to take a break and enjoy some local delicacies.

For a unique experience, visit the Sainte-Croix Lake, located at the entrance of the Gorges du Verdon. This artificial lake is perfect for swimming, paddleboarding, or simply relaxing by the water's edge. The surrounding mountains add to the lake’s serene beauty.
