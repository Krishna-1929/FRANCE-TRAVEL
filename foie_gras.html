Foie Gras

Foie Gras, a luxurious and iconic French dish, is made from the liver of a duck or goose. Known for its smooth texture and rich, delicate flavor, it is a staple of French fine dining and is often served during holidays and special occasions.

Foie Gras is typically served as a pâté or mousse on toast or paired with a sweet, fruity compote or wine. Its melt-in-your-mouth texture and deep flavor make it an unforgettable culinary experience for anyone who appreciates French gastronomy.

Whether you try it in a traditional French bistro or as part of a gourmet tasting menu, Foie Gras is a must-try for anyone visiting France. The combination of luxury, craftsmanship, and centuries of tradition make it one of the country's most celebrated dishes.
