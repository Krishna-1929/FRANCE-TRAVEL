Experience at Pont du Gard

Pont du Gard, located near Nîmes in the south of France, is a remarkable ancient Roman aqueduct bridge. This UNESCO World Heritage site, built in the 1st century AD, stands as a testament to Roman engineering and architecture. The bridge spans the Gardon River and was originally part of an aqueduct that supplied water to the Roman city of Nîmes. Today, Pont du Gard offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore one of the best-preserved ancient monuments in France.

Pont du Gard is an extraordinary feat of engineering, with its three tiers of arches spanning the Gardon River. The bridge rises to 49 meters, making it one of the tallest Roman structures still standing. Its impressive size and beauty make it an iconic symbol of ancient Roman civilization.

The aqueduct once carried water across the region, and its precise construction is a marvel of Roman architecture. The bridge's arches were designed to withstand the force of the river's flow while maintaining the structure's stability over time.

Visitors can walk along the bridge and take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape, including the Gardon River and lush countryside. The site also offers a museum where you can learn more about the history and significance of the Pont du Gard, as well as its role in the development of ancient Rome's water supply systems.

The museum at Pont du Gard provides fascinating exhibits on the construction of the aqueduct and the lives of the Romans who built it. You can discover the ancient tools and techniques used to build the aqueduct, as well as the story of how the bridge has stood the test of time.

For a unique experience, take a kayak or boat ride along the Gardon River, which flows beneath the bridge. This offers an unforgettable view of the Pont du Gard from below and allows visitors to fully appreciate the scale and grandeur of this ancient monument.
