Discover Lyon

Lyon, a UNESCO World Heritage city, is France's gastronomic capital and a hub of history, culture, and stunning architecture. Known for its Renaissance old town, vibrant arts scene, and exceptional food, Lyon is a must-visit destination for travelers looking to explore French heritage and enjoy delicious cuisine.

The panoramic view of Lyon from Fourvière Hill offers stunning vistas of the city, showcasing its blend of modern and historical architecture. At the top stands the Basilique Notre-Dame de Fourvière, offering breathtaking views.

The Old Town (Vieux Lyon) is one of the most picturesque areas in the city, with its cobbled streets, Renaissance buildings, and hidden traboules (passageways) that once served as shortcuts for silk workers.

Lyon is famed for its culinary scene, often regarded as the food capital of France. Local specialties like "quenelles" (dumplings), "salade lyonnaise" (Lyonnaise salad), and "tarte à la praline" can be enjoyed at local bouchons.

Lyon's Festival of Lights (Fête des Lumières) is a magical event held every December, where buildings and streets come to life with artistic light displays, drawing thousands of visitors each year.
